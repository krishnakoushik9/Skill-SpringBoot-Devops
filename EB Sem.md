### Unit V: Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

#### Definition of CSR

- World Business Council for Sustainable Development: CSR involves ethical behavior and contributions to economic development, improving the quality of life for employees, families, local communities, and society.
- Archie Carroll (1991): CSR is a multi-layered concept with four aspects: economic, legal, ethical, and philanthropic responsibilities.

#### Pillars of Corporate Sustainability

- Economic Sustainability
- Social Sustainability
- Environmental Sustainability
    - Collectively known as the 'triple bottom line.'

#### Types of CSR

- Environmental Responsibility: Reducing pollution, recycling, and sustainable practices.
- Ethical Responsibility: Fair treatment, transparency, and ethical business practices.
- Philanthropic Responsibility: Donations, supporting charities, and community involvement.
- Financial Responsibility: Investing in sustainable and ethical initiatives.

#### CSR Activities

- Eradicating hunger and poverty
- Promoting education and gender equality
- Improving maternal health and combating diseases
- Ensuring environmental sustainability
- Enhancing vocational skills and social business projects

#### Advantages of CSR

- Enhanced brand image and reputation
- Customer loyalty and trust
- Employee satisfaction and retention
- Operational cost savings
- Attracting investors
- Risk management
- Innovation and improvement

#### CSR and Corporate Governance

- Relationship: Good governance is about values, and CSR is an external expression of those values.
- Models for Implementation of CSR:
    - Carroll’s Model of CSR: Economic, legal, ethical, and philanthropic responsibilities.
    - Triple Bottom Line (TBL): People, Planet, and Profit.

#### Steps to Attain CSR

- Commitment from top management
- Stakeholder engagement
- Assessing impact
- Setting goals and targets
- Integration into business strategy
- Implementation and monitoring
- Transparency and reporting
- Continuous improvement

#### Business Council for Sustainable Development (BCSD) India

- Collaboration and partnership for sustainable business strategies.
- Focus on sustainability and global network involvement.

#### Social Responsibility and Indian Corporations

- Key Drivers of CSR in India:
    - Regulatory mandates
    - Consumer expectations
    - Government initiatives
    - Global standards
    - Environmental sustainability
    - Corporate reputation
    - Stakeholder pressure
    - Socio-economic needs
    - Employee engagement

#### Top Companies Leading CSR in India

- Tata Group, Reliance Industries, Wipro, Infosys, ITC Limited.

#### CSR as a Business Strategy for Sustainable Development

- Creating shared value for shareholders and stakeholders.
- Best practices for integrating CSR:
    - Setting ambitious CSR targets
    - Appointing a CSR Director
    - Tying CSR to executive pay
    - Embedding CSR into all functions
    - Issuing annual CSR reports
    - Creating internal CSR networks

#### CSR Drivers

- Enlightened self-interest
- Social investment
- Transparency and trust
