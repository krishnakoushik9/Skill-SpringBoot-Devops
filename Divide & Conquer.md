# basic integration functions

The **basic integration functions** and formulas are foundational to calculus and frequently appear in problems involving indefinite integrals (antiderivatives). Here are the most important ones:

- **Power Rule:**

$$
\int x^n dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)
$$

- **Integral of a Constant:**

$$
\int a\, dx = ax + C
$$

- **Reciprocal (Natural Logarithm):**

$$
\int \frac{1}{x} dx = \ln|x| + C
$$

- **Exponential Functions:**
    - Base $e$:

$$
\int e^{x} dx = e^{x} + C
$$
    - Base $a$:

$$
\int a^{x} dx = \frac{a^{x}}{\ln a} + C
$$


- **Trigonometric Functions:**
    - Sine and Cosine:

$$
\int \sin x\, dx = -\cos x + C
$$

$$
\int \cos x\, dx = \sin x + C
$$
- Tangent and Cotangent:

$$
\int \tan x\, dx = -\ln|\cos x| + C
$$

$$
\int \cot x\, dx = \ln|\sin x| + C
$$
- Secant and Cosecant:

$$
\int \sec^2 x\, dx = \tan x + C
$$

$$
\int \csc^2 x\, dx = -\cot x + C
$$

- **Inverse Trigonometric Functions:**

$$
\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} dx = \sin^{-1} x + C
$$

$$
\int \frac{1}{1+x^2} dx = \tan^{-1} x + C
$$

$$
\int \frac{1}{x\sqrt{x^2-1}} dx = \sec^{-1} x + C
$$

**General rules** to remember:

- $\int [f(x) + g(x)] dx = \int f(x) dx + \int g(x) dx$
- $\int a f(x) dx = a \int f(x) dx$ for constant $a$[^6][^9]
